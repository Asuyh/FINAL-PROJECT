What comes to mind when you hear the word "technology"? It might sound like something out of a science fiction film or something that needs to be powered by electricity. Technology tends to make us think of the extremely complicated, but energy does not have to be.

Technology is the application of scientific knowledge to real-world problems, whether in industry or in our daily lives. In other words, whenever we apply our scientific knowledge to a specific goal, we are applying technology. There's a little more to it than that, though. Technology usually entails the use of a certain piece of equipment, which might be either extremely simple or dazzlingly sophisticated. It might be anything from the invention of the wheel to computers and MP3 players.

Technology has advanced at a breakneck pace in recent decades. In addition, the use of technology has skyrocketed. It has an impact on people's lives and alters how they learn, think, and communicate. It has become increasingly important in society, and it is difficult to fathom living without it. Technology and society are intertwined, interdependent, and impact one another. In both good and harmful ways, technology has an impact on society, including the potential for civilization to grow or decline. Technology has influenced our culture, with both positive and negative implications. Human societies and technology have become intricately intertwined, as technological systems such as cell phones, computers, television, and other media are created by humans and reflect the fundamental foundation of a society.Although technology enriches human lives, it is also a huge source of concern for future generations. People nowadays use technology excessively, which reduces their physical activity, which has a direct impact on their health. Also, due to the widespread use of technology, a large number of cybercrimes occur every day in which someone steals the victim's identity or personal information, such as their Unique ID number, PAN number, or Debit Card, and uses it to commit a crime or commit fraud without their permission.
